# Three dimensions

## Statement

The spatial dimension of the framework is

$$d \;=\; 3,$$

determined (not assumed) by self-consistent adjacency of the
[mediant](mediant.md) under its continuum completion.  Equivalently,
$d = \dim \mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R})$.

## Proof

The argument has four steps, each of them standard once the framework
axioms are in place.

### 1. The mediant is the column-sum action of $\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{Z})$

Write the pair $(a/b,\, c/d)$ as the matrix

$$M \;=\; \begin{pmatrix} a & c \\ b & d \end{pmatrix}.$$

The mediant $a/b \oplus c/d = (a+c)/(b+d)$ is the column-sum of $M$.
Two fractions are Farey neighbors iff $|ad - bc| = 1$, i.e. iff
$\det M = \pm 1$.  This is the defining condition of
$\mathrm{GL}(2, \mathbb{Z})$; restricting to $\det M = +1$ (the
orientation of the Farey ordering) gives $\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{Z})$.

### 2. The continuum completion is $\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R})$

The mediant acts on $\mathbb{Q} \cup \{\infty\}$, which is the rational
boundary of the upper half plane $\mathbb{H}^2$.  Completing denominators
$b, d$ to the reals and extending the action by continuity gives the
standard $\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R})$ action on $\mathbb{H}^2$:

$$\begin{pmatrix} a & c \\ b & d \end{pmatrix} \cdot z
  \;=\; \frac{az + c}{bz + d}, \qquad z \in \mathbb{H}^2.$$

No new assumption is used: continuity is the minimal extension
preserving the Farey ordering.

### 3. Self-consistent adjacency forces the manifold to be the group

The coupling on the Stern-Brocot tree induces a graph on rational
points: $p/q$ and $p'/q'$ are adjacent iff they are Farey neighbors.
Under the continuum limit, this graph becomes an adjacency
structure on a manifold $M$, and the [fixed-point](fixed_point.md)
axiom demands that $M$ be closed under its own adjacency:

> **Self-consistent adjacency.**  The adjacency relation on $M$ induced
> by the mediant is a fixed point of the adjacency-generating action
> of $\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R})$.

For a manifold acted on transitively by a connected Lie group $G$ with
isotropy group $H$, the Klein-geometric representation gives
$M \cong G / H$.  The self-consistency requirement forces $H$ to be
trivial: if $H$ were nontrivial, an element of $H$ would fix a point
of $M$, breaking the "every adjacency is generated by a unique group
element" condition that the mediant establishes.  Therefore
$M \cong G$ itself:

$$M \;\cong\; \mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R}).$$

### 4. Dimension count

$\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R})$ is the set of $2 \times 2$ real matrices
with $\det = 1$, a codimension-one submanifold of $\mathbb{R}^4$:

$$\dim \mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R}) \;=\; 4 - 1 \;=\; 3.$$

The three directions correspond to the Iwasawa decomposition
$KAN$: a compact rotation $K = \mathrm{SO}(2)$, a hyperbolic boost
$A$, and a parabolic shift $N$.  These are the three spatial
directions of the framework.

## Remarks

- **No continuum postulate.**  The $3$ is forced by the Lie-group
  structure of the mediant's continuum completion, not by a
  topological assumption on space.
- **Signature.**  This theorem fixes the spatial dimension only.  The
  time direction enters via complexification
  $\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R}) \to \mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{C})$ with
  the corresponding signature $(3, 1)$;
  see [`minkowski_signature`](minkowski_signature.md).
- **Uniqueness.**  The Bianchi classification of three-dimensional Lie
  groups singles out $\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R})$ as the unique simple
  non-compact three-dimensional Lie group with the relevant structure;
  see [`lie_group_characterization`](lie_group_characterization.md).

## Corollaries

- Spatial dimension is not a free parameter.
- The four known forces fit into a [Klein-bottle](klein_bottle.md)
  quotient of $\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R}) \times \mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R})$
  via the antiperiodic identification.
- The [duty function](duty_function.md) exponent $d = 3$ is the
  dimension of this same Lie group, not a coincidence.
