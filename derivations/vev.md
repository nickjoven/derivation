# Electroweak vacuum anchor

## Axiom (observational)

The particle-physics sector takes one dimensional input: the Higgs
vacuum expectation value,

$$v \;=\; 246.22 \ \mathrm{GeV},$$

derived from the Fermi constant $G_F = 1/(\sqrt{2}\,v^2)$ measured in
muon decay.  Equivalently, the electroweak scale or the root
oscillator frequency $\omega_0 = v/\hbar$ may be substituted.

No attempt to derive $v$ from the framework's mathematical
primitives has succeeded; this entry states it as given.

## Covers

Given $v$, the framework expresses the following as dimensionless
identities or in natural units:

- Absolute masses $m_H$, $m_W$, $m_Z$, and charged-lepton masses —
  through dimensionless mass ratios set by Klein-bottle integers and
  [generation mechanism](generation_mechanism.md) exponents.
- $\hbar$, $c$, $G$ as absolute SI quantities — through the
  identifications $\hbar = v \cdot t_v$, $c = 1$ in natural units,
  $G$ from $\ell_P = \sqrt{\hbar G / c^3}$ combined with the
  [hierarchy](hierarchy.md) $R = M_P / M_H$.

## Status

Observational anchor.  Independent of the cosmological anchor
[`hubble`](hubble.md).  The dimensionless ratio $v/M_P \approx 2 \times
10^{-17}$ has no derivation in the framework; see
[`v_over_mp_gap`](v_over_mp_gap.md).  Anchor count is therefore two,
not one.
